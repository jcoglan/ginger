# kuuee

Each part of speech symbol -- 'sj', 'k', 'hh', etc -- appears in one definition
with type 'sj' where the symbol is followed by the word 'kuuee'. The symbol is
always the first 1-2 letters of the word being defined, i.e. its abbreviation.

    agappx [2, sj]   = ewer z kytu. a kuuee.
    buhhtes [1, sj]  = yre eeluz ahk subtet ee subtat kyin vx yra. bu kuuee.
    cjdacx [0, sj]   = deqhm z sjdr. cj kuuee.
    hhgappx [0, sj]  = eguux in agappx. hh kuuee.
    idocxl [0, sj]   = yra luwux qwq deqhm. i kuuee.
    kytu [85, sj]    = kyv in sxresh in hgasi kyv f gega. k kuuee.
    pgappx [0, sj]   = yre pul. p kuuee.
    qvdedein [0, sj] = hgasi z qvq in akudep kyv f gega. q kuuee.
    rpqata [0, sj]   = vzt in sjlup in hgasi beax f gega. r kuuee.
    sjdr [4, sj]     = hucher deqhm f gega. sj kuuee.
    yutehe [2, sj]   = zuf deqhm. y kuuee.

    eguux [15, sj] =
        1: pdepr ee btebr f xihuhesh bteb f ewer in eishu. ahk qhh o gega shlp.
        2: lela in deda bteb f gega. e kuuee.

This tells us the names for all the parts of speech. Note that some are defined
as combinations of other categories, or in relation to them:

- 'agappx' = 'ewer z kytu'
- 'cjdacx' = 'deqhm z sjdr'
- 'hhgappx' = 'eguux in agappx'

This suggests that 'agappx' is a modifier of 'kytu', i.e. an adverb, although
there are relatively few of them (41). Similarly, 'cjdacx' means a word combined
with nouns, so an adjective or preposition. 'cj' has only 15 members so
preposition is more likely; we already believe 'eguux' means 'adjective'.

'buhhtes' talks about the 'previous and next' of something and likely means
'conjunction', which is consistent with the ones we know so far.

'z' could mean 'of' or 'for'; it is very common and it certainly does something
to relate the previous and next words:

    z [227, r] = subtet subtat dkdg deqhm. ulu subtat subtet ji.

It would make some sense for 'hhgappx' to mean things that are both adjectives
and adverbs, i.e. descriptive/property terms.

'kytu' also has a 'k' definition:

    kytu [85]
        k.
            1: kyv kysi.
            2: kakauka kyso.

There's a lot of words beginning 'ky' here and indeed all the words beginning
'ky' seem interrelated, most of them being verbs, possibly all such words are
related to verbs, action, doing, etc.

    kyao [1, k]   = ju yra yra kyin el je kyso.

    kyin [77, k]  = 1: suba hji am subt ji.
                    2: suba heba am subt beba.

    kysa [1, k]   = vh yra yra kysi.

    kysi [42, k]  = subtatef sxresh kytu.

    kyso [29, k]  = vk yra kysi.

    kyta [2, k]   = ehu ehg yra gudde kka kytu. hytu aohh ehu ehg yra kap gudde.

    kytu [85, k]  = 1: kyv kysi.
                    2: kakauka kyso.
    kytu [85, sj] = kyv in sxresh in hgasi kyv f gega. k kuuee.

    kyv [35, k]   = bteb in pdep.
    kyv [35, sj]  = lela in deda ahk kytu f kusxkusx deda.

Two further definitions of verbs use the word 'kuuee', including itself:

    kuuee [14]
        k.
            axuax gega uxaux gega kelio. aokasx e eguux kuuee.

    kelio [5]
        k.
            1: axuax lela uxaux lela kuuee.
            2: gega in heha uxaux eeluz in kusxkusx xihuhesh kuuee.

Note how these both include the form 'axuax N uxaux N V' where N and V are
either 'gega'/'kelio' or 'lela'/'kuuee'. 'axuax' and 'uxaux' are possibly
opposites (c.f. 'kap' notes), and 'kuuee' is used to relate an abbreviation like
'bu' to its full word 'buhhtes'. So 'axuax' and 'uxaux' could mean big/small or
long/short.
