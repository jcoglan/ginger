# kap

There are many pairs of words that are related very simply by the word 'kap',
one often being defined as 'X = kap Y'.

    shg [19, k] = helu je kytu am taar in tair yra ji.
    xhg [5, k]  = kap shg.

'kap' itself is defined as:

    kap [132, hh] = xsiga kyv.
                    <sj>  <sj|k>

Most of the words related in this way seem to be 'sj', 'k', or 'e', which we
think are nouns, verbs, and adjectives. A great many of them involve the word
'kytu':

    kysi [42, k] = subtatef sxresh kytu.
    kyso [29, k] = vk yra kysi.
    xysi [1, k]  = kap uxi kysi.

    kytu [85, k]  = 1: kyv kysi. 2: kakauka kyso.
    kytu [85, sj] = kyv in sxresh in hgasi kyv f gega. k kuuee.
    hytu [6, k]   = kap kytu.

    lll [3, k]   = kauin je hji el je bteb.
    lav [9, sj]  = ahk tta lela kytu.
    lav [9, e]   = yre ji. ju yra hkdg. lava. lavit. kap lll.
    eut [1, e]   = kap lav.
    gude [2, e]  = cxla ahk uxaux lela kytu.
    ehut [15, e] = kap lav ee kap gude. uxauxf in axuaxf ahk.

    ahk [121, k] = ging dkdg vk kytu.
    hhk [45, k]  = kap ahk.

    invh [3, a] = kytu vs qvq.
    inxh [2, a] = kap invh.

    airlx [5, k] = suba kytu am subb hytu.
    airli [5, k] = suba kytu aa subb kytu aa ehg kytu.
    irli [1, a]  = airli. kap airlx.

    qrfde [6, e]    = kap kusxkusx. aokasx vzte deda.
    qrfde [6, a]    = subb kytu.
    kusxkusx [9, e] = kap qrfde.

    ska [14, k] = exate kytu.
    iska [2, e] = ahk in belu ska.
    xska [1, e] = kap iska.

    eytu [1, e] = ehut el yra kytu ji.
    xytu [1, e] = kap eytu.

    axhk [8, k] = 1: vk ju yra ee yra ysi hucher kytu.
                  2: ysi hucher yre hucher kyin.
    yre [96, y] = kap axhk f yra deqhm.

In a few cases, the word defined as 'kap X' replaces the first letter of X with
'h', or prefixes X with 'h'; see 'hytu' and 'hhk' above, plus:

    ahg [35, k] = vk gudde aa shg dkdg.
    hhg [13, k] = kap ahg.

    akudep [45, k]  = plu guo rah in dyt.
    akudep [45, sj] = qvq z guo.
    hkudep [5, k]   = kap akudep.

    asj [50, k] = 1: vk ja je f akudep kyv.
                  2: ahk yamte ew el lela chley.
                  3: yra belu rak.
    hsj [4, k] = kap asj.

    beba [35, k] = ging ahk pdep. yra yra ji.
    heba [18, k] = kap beba.

    belu [48, k] = plu akudep plaf.
    helu [9, k]  = kap belu.

    dkdg [119, k] = wux z yra ju yra kadu.
    hkdg [30, k]  = kap dkdg.

    ji [308, k] = 1: beba.
                  2: qrexzt deqhm.
    hji [65, k] = kap ji.

    kadu [55, k] = vh yra ji.
    hadu [5, k]  = kap kadu.

So many words defined as simply 'kap X' indicates 'kap' is some sort of
modifier, or logical/relational term. It's particularly interesting that some
pairs of words are defined as each one being 'kap' of the other, for example:

    qrfde [6, e]    = kap kusxkusx. aokasx vzte deda.
    kusxkusx [9, e] = kap qrfde.

    axuax [36, hh] = kap uxaux. zuf bteb.
    uxaux [79, hh] = kap axuax. zuf bteb.

    kauin [34, e] = 1: axuax ging dkdg. kap taar.
                    2: exiex axuaxf ging dkdg. kap taar.
    taar [41, e]  = uxaux ging dkdg. kap kauin.

This suggests 'kap' is not an ordering relation where one word denotes something
bigger/more intense than the other, or these cyclic references would make no
sense. What would make sense is for 'kap' to indicate negation, inversion or
opposition. However, it can't indicate a binary opposite since there are sets of
words where A is 'kap B', B is 'kap A', and C is 'kap A ee kap B'.

    lav [9, e]   = yre ji. ju yra hkdg. lava. lavit. kap lll.
    gude [2, e]  = cxla ahk uxaux lela kytu.
    ehut [15, e] = kap lav ee kap gude. uxauxf in axuaxf ahk.

    axuax [36, hh] = kap uxaux. zuf bteb.
    uxaux [79, hh] = kap axuax. zuf bteb.
    exiex [23, hh] = kap axuax ee kap uxaux.

    kauin [34, e] = 1: axuax ging dkdg. kap taar.
                    2: exiex axuaxf ging dkdg. kap taar.
    taar [41, e]  = uxaux ging dkdg. kap kauin.
    eiar[1, hh]   = kap taar ee kap kauin.

    subt [17, sj] = kap subteta ee kap subtate.

So if 'kap' does mean 'not' in the sense of negation or opposition or exclusion,
then there are words defined as 'not A and not B'; these definitions suggest
'ee' means 'and'.

This gives us 'hji' meaning 'to not be' and 'hadu' meaning 'not be in' or 'not
contain' depending on what 'kadu' means.

Bear in mind that one definition of 'ji' is just 'beba' while 'beba' (k) is
defined as 'ging ahk pdep. yra yra ji.' Possibly 'ji' and 'beba' are different
tenses or gerunds or some such.

There are some words that seem to be defined as 'X or not X', for example:

    pji [6, k] = ji in hji.

    suerr [3]
        e.
            beba in heba deqhm.
        k.
            beba in heba.
        sj.
            hgasi z beba in heba deqhm.

It's not immediately clear what this means, or if these are just contradictions
that means we're wrong about what 'kap' and 'in' mean. Another seeming
contradiction is:

    liuzu [16, sj] = vh wux ee wax. am kap vh wux ee wax.

'wux' and 'wax' are seemingly opposites ('hkdg = kap dkdg'):

    wux [17, e] = liuzu pdepr ee btebr hkdg.
    wax [10, e] = liuzu pdepr ee btebr dkdg.

These phrases aren't necessarily nonsensical, it's not uncommon to see 'X and
<opposite of X>' in normal usage.
