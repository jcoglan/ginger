# subtet and subtat

From the controls, we know that 'subtet{a,a,af}' and 'subtat{,e,ef}' refer to
notions of going backwards or forwards in space or time. If we also have 'kap'
meaning 'not' and 'ee' meaning 'and', then 'subt' seems to mean 'not before and
not after', could be something like 'centre' or 'current position' or 'here' or
'now'.

    subt [17, k] =
        sj.
            kap subteta ee kap subtate.
        hh.
            kap vh subteta ee kap vh subtate.


## suba, subb and ehg

The words 'suba', 'subb' and 'ehg' refer to performing the verb 'kyv' either
before (in the past?), here/now, or after (the future?):

    suba [49, k] = vh subteta kyv.
    subb [49, k] = subt kyv.
    ehg [27, k]  = vh subtate kyv.

Many definitions use some combination of these separated by 'am'. They're often
combined with opposites like 'ji'/'hji', 'beba', 'heba', 'kytu'/'hytu',
'dkdg'/'hkdg', etc.

    eji [9, k]   = ehg ji. am subb hji.

    gudde [26, k] = qvq suba yra ji. am heha ehg yra ji.
    gudda [1, e]  = suba gudde.

    aidu [1, k] = qah suba kadu am subb kap kadu.
    aik [6, k]  = ju yra suba kadu o yra subb kadu.

    airlx [5, k] = suba kytu am subb hytu.
    airli [5, k] = suba kytu aa subb kytu aa ehg kytu.
    irli [1, a]  = airli. kap airlx.

    alp [15, e] = hucher alpu. suba heba in bteb am subb beba in bteb. kakauka
    vs du ju yra ji.

    aoliga [2, k] = suba hkdg am subb dkdg. rak.

    gzg [1, a] = suba lll am olla subb ji.

    hjira [1, k] = yras suba shlup o subb axht.

    kesdat [0, k] = qwq suba dkdg am subb hkdg.
    kesdet [1, k] = qah suba kadu am subb hadu.

    kvin [2, k]   = qlate suba ji am subb hji.

    kyin [77, k]  = 1: suba hji am subt ji.
                    2: suba heba am subt beba.

    likaz [0, e]  = suba heba am subb beba.

    rah [5, k] = riju suba ji am qahhi subb ji.

    rcxs [3, k] = suba dkdg am subb hkdg.

    sjam [1, sj] = egaxi suba ji am subb hji f yra.

    tkr [8, k] = pul suba ji am subb hji.

    tku [11, k] = suba kap lqa am vx law subb tky.

    tky [15, k] = gudi suba hji am subb ji.

    ubva [21, k] = suba ji o subb yre ji.

    xradu [0, k] = ju lela suba lela asj am subb kesdet.

    ysi [36, y] = ju yre yre.
    ysi [36, a] = 1: kytu o siuaa kytu.
                  2: suba ji lpubal hji am subb ji.

These constructions seem like they might describe a change or discontinuity of
some kind. For example 'suba hji am subt ji' could mean 'not be in the past, but
now be'. One of the definitions of 'ji' is just 'beba', and a short definition
of 'to be' is just 'to exist', so 'beba' could mean 'exist'. Then 'suba heba am
subt beba' would mean 'not exist in the past, but now exist'. So 'kyin' could
mean 'start being' or 'start existing', i.e. 'begin' or 'appear'. If the 'subt'
words also related to space, then 'kyin' could also relate to something
beginning at a certain point in space.

We can make some educated guesses at other words above:

- 'eji' means to be something in the future.

- 'aidu': 'qah suba kadu am subb kap kadu': We don't know 'qah' yet but the rest
  of the phrase means to be in something in the past but not be in it now, i.e.
  to leave.

- 'aik': 'ju yra suba kadu o yra subb kadu': We've mostly seen 'ju' in the
  numbers to indicate adding two numbers together and it's possible it means a
  more general concept of combining things. The rest of the phrase indicates
  being in something both now and in the past i.e. to remain.

- 'airlx' means to stop doing or being the verb/noun 'kytu'. We know 'kytu'
  means 'verb' or maybe 'action' so this just means stop doing, or cease'.

- 'airli' is some conjunction 'aa' combining the verb 'kytu' in the past, present
  and future. Maybe this describes an ongoing action, the present participle,
  etc. i.e. it just means 'doing'.

- 'irli' means 'airli' but not 'airlx', i.e. continue doing something and not
  stop.

- 'alp' has something to do with coming into being 'in bteb'. Possibly related
  to noun 'alpp' for 'start/birth'.

- 'aoliga' means to begin doing the verb 'dkdg'.

- 'kesdat' means to stop doing the verb 'dkdg' in relation to the noun 'qwq'.

- 'kesdet' means to stop being in the noun 'qah'.

- 'kvin' means to stop being the noun 'qlate'.

- 'likaz' means an adjective related to start existing. It could mean 'new' but
  this seems too common a word to have no uses.

- 'rah' means to be adjective 'riju' in the past but to now be adjective
  'qahhi'.

- 'rcxs' means to stop doing the verb 'dkdg', i.e. the opposite of 'aoliga'.

- 'sjam' is a noun that is adjective 'egaxi' in the past but not now.

- 'tkr' means to stop being the noun 'pul'.

- 'tky' means to begin being the adjective 'gudi'.
